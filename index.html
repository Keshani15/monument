About Stonehenge

Stonehenge is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most famous archaeological sites in the world. It consists of a circular arrangement of large standing stones, some weighing up to 25 tons, set within earthworks. The monument's construction dates back to around 3000 BCE to 2000 BCE, though various phases of development occurred over centuries.
Learn more

The exact purpose of Stonehenge remains a subject of speculation but one cannot deny the overwhelming sense of awe when one visits it! The sheer size and antiquity of Stonehenge makes one feel "small". One often feels a deep connection to the past when standing amidst the ancient stones. The realization that they are in the presence of a structure built thousands of years ago can be a profound and humbling experience.
